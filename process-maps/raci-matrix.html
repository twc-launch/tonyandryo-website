💡 Key Insights from the RACI

⚖️
Lead is heavier than Support, by design. The Lead owns the creative core (script, edit, title, thumbnail concept). Support handles logistics and QA. This is healthy — the person with the creative vision should have decision authority.
🤖
Ali already covers 16 tasks (~36% of all work). Most of this is in Distribution/Growth and Packaging. The biggest automatable wins: translations, analytics, community posts, newsletters, and idea board maintenance. These used to be intern tasks or fell through the cracks.
🚨
Every gate decision has the Lead as Accountable. No ambiguity about who makes the call. Support is always Consulted at gates — they have input, but Lead decides. This prevents decision deadlock.
📦
6 tasks are marked outsourceable. The editing block (main edit, subs, B-roll insert, color) is the biggest outsourceable module. A single video editor could take 3-4 tasks off the Lead's plate. Thumbnail design and Shorts cutting are also clean handoff points.
⚠️
Production phase is all-human. No automation opportunity during filming. This is the most time-intensive phase and the hardest to scale — which is exactly why every other phase should be as efficient as possible.
🔄
No task has zero ownership. Every row has at least one R. This is the "leakage prevention" the matrix provides — if you can't assign someone, the task either doesn't need to exist or you've found a gap.
