🚧 Stage-Gate Decision Points

Each gate is a deliberate go/no-go checkpoint. Lead makes the call; support gives input.

Gate 1 — Idea Worth Pursuing?

After Ideation → Before Pre-Prod

Criteria: Does this serve our audience? Is the angle unique? Can we add real value? Does it fit the content calendar?

✓ GO → Plan it ↻ Backlog ✕ Kill

Gate 2 — Script & Plan Solid?

After Pre-Prod → Before Shoot

Criteria: Is the hook strong? Is the structure clear? Do we have all research/data? Is the shot list ready?

✓ GO → Shoot ↻ Rework outline ✕ Shelve

Gate 3 — Footage Usable?

After Production → Before Edit

Criteria: Audio clean? All talking points covered? B-roll sufficient? Any reshoot needed?

✓ GO → Edit ↻ Reshoot parts ✕ Rare

Gate 4 — Edit Approved?

After Post-Prod → Before Packaging

Criteria: Pacing good? Story arc holds? Subtitles accurate? Watch-through complete?

✓ GO → Package ↻ Revision notes ✕ Very rare

Gate 5 — Thumbnail & Title Ready?

After Packaging → Before Publish

Criteria: Passes 18% mobile test? Curiosity gap present? Title/thumb combo compelling? Multi-lang metadata done?

✓ GO → Publish ↻ Rework thumb/title

Gate 6 — Post-Launch Review

7 days after Publish

Criteria: CTR vs benchmark? AVD holding? Shorts performance? Community engagement? Anything to fix?

✓ Archive & learn ↻ Update thumb/title

📌 How the Lead/Support Model Works

  • Each video has one Lead and one Support. The Lead drives the creative vision, writes/outlines, does the main CapCut edit, and makes final calls at each gate.
  • Lead and Support are decided per project — not fixed roles. Tony leads some videos; Ryo leads others. This is intentional and allows both to own content.
  • The Support handles supplementary work: B-roll filming, subtitle creation, research assistance, upload logistics, community engagement.
  • Gate decisions default to the Lead, but both discuss. If they disagree, the Lead's call stands for their video.
  • Interns (Harry, Ria) currently handle some Distribution tasks. When they depart, those tasks migrate to the Support role or Ali (OpenClaw) for automatable items.
  • The 🤖 Ali row shows what's automatable today or near-term. Translations, analytics, newsletter drafts, SEO checks, and idea board maintenance are all within current capability.
  • The 📦 Outsourceable row shows detachable modules — self-contained tasks you could hand to a contractor without them needing deep context. These are your "plug and play" zones for scaling.
